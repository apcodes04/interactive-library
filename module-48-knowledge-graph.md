# Module 48 — Knowledge Graph Integration (Q38 — LLM Question)

Build a clickable node-link graph with a fact side-panel. Use D3.js force-directed graph, or `vis-network`/`Cytoscape.js` if preferred. Follow `00-design-system.md` for colors/type/motion.

**Exact data — 7 entity nodes and edges:**
- Paris —capital_of→ France
- Eiffel Tower —located_in→ Paris
- Eiffel Tower —built_in→ 1889
- Louvre —located_in→ Paris
- Seine —flows_through→ Paris
- France —part_of→ Europe
- Paris —on_river→ Seine

**Layout:** a `.card` with the 7 nodes (`--primary` circles, labeled) connected by labeled edges (`--text-muted` lines with small text labels), plus a side panel.

**Behavior:** clicking any node highlights it (`--active` amber) and its directly connected edges, and populates the side panel with each connected fact as a plain-language sentence, generated by templating the edge label (e.g. "Paris is the capital of France," "The Eiffel Tower is located in Paris," "The Eiffel Tower was built in 1889") rather than hardcoding sentences per node.

**Caption:** "Click 'Eiffel Tower' — every connected edge becomes a plain sentence instantly. That's the graph-to-text conversion step described in this module, made concrete."

**Steps to interact (render as `.steps-panel`, visible on load):**
1. Click any entity node.
2. Read the plain-language facts that appear in the side panel.
